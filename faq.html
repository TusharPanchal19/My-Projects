Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. What is blood donation?

Blood donation is the voluntary process of giving blood, used for transfusions and medical treatment.

2. How much blood is taken during donation?

About 350-450 ml of blood is typically collected per donation.

3. How can I prepare for donating blood?

Drink plenty of water, eat a healthy meal before donation, and get a good night's rest.

4. Who can donate blood?

Generally, healthy individuals aged 18–65 weighing over 50 kg can donate.

5. How often can I donate blood?

You can donate whole blood every 3 months (90 days).

6. Is blood donation safe?

Yes. New, sterile equipment is used for each donor, making the process completely safe.

7. Can I donate blood if I am on medication?

It depends on the medication. Consult with your doctor or the donation center first.

8. Will I feel weak after donating blood?

Some people may feel a little dizzy briefly, but with rest and hydration, you'll feel fine quickly.

9. How long does the donation process take?

The whole process, including paperwork and rest time, takes about 45 minutes to 1 hour.

10. Where can I donate blood?

You can donate at government hospitals, Red Cross centers, private blood banks, or donation camps.
